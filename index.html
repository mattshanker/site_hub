Responsive Web Design

Media queries, adaptive content and grid layout allow your site to respond to fit any client's screen. Now customers don't have have to pinch and zoom your desktop site. Get the feel of an app without the app store.

Dynamic vs Static

Dyanmic sites use a content management system, like WordPress, to generate and keep track of content on your site. Your site starts as HTML, CSS, and JS files, and gradually grows as more content is created. Static sites are built and served using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files. Content updates to these site come from making changes to these files directly. If you don't need to change you content more than 1 or 2 times a month and would rather have a more secure and maintainable site, a static site is the best value.

Landing pages - your site's welcome mat

What happens after someone clicks on your ad? Or your email blast?

Ever clicked on a company's ad or email and then get sent to a page that makes you feel lost or disoriented? That's where a good landing page can convert someone's interest into further action, like buying or subscribing. They should be tailored to the specific customer group they're targeting (Welcome to our site, readers of Pitbull Magazine!) and make someone feel at home.
