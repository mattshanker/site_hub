Responsive Web Design

As the internet has become something you carry with you instead of something you go to, websites have had to work hard to keep up. Mobile internet access is the main way many people get on the internet today and responsive web design has emerged to link the laptop/desktop past with the smartphone future. Media queries, adaptive content and grid layout let your site respond to fit any client's screen no matter whether it's desk or pocket size.

Performance impacts usability

One of the most important measurements for any website is speed. The speed that a page renders on a user's device depends on factors like network traffic but page size is the most controllable element of website speed, as perceived by your users. As of April 2015, Google's search algorithm has already started penalizing websites that aren't optimized for mobile in results. But more important is the perception your users get if your site loads slowly or reacts unexpectedly on mobile: They'll think your site is broken and go somewhere else.
