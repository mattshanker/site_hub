I started in web development after I realized marketing was considered a commodity discipline, and decided I wanted to make things instead. So, I decided to build a weather app for my Android phone based on the DarkSky API. After taking a week to install the Eclipse environment and the Android SDK, I abandoned the idea of an app altogether, and pivoted to websites. Not because the task was too challenging (okay, maybe a little) but because websites can be accessed without an App Store, on any device. After spending time learning the basics of web development, I decided to make it my career.

Why Freelance?

Tell people that you're a freelancer and they think you're just clever about being unemployed. The truth is that for organizations of a certain size, freelancers offer the best value and balance between an agency that might see the job as too small to be worth it, and someone who may have made a webpage or two in the past by may lack knowledge of modern standards or professionalism.

I work to keep informed of the latest techniques and bring that knowledge to each and every project I take on, giving you piece of mind that when you hire me, you're hiring someone who is up to date on the latest in modern web development.
