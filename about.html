A marketer by major, I joined the web development community after I became disenchanted with marketing and tried to build a weather app for my Android phone based on the DarkSky API. After taking a week to install the Eclipse program and the Android SDK, I abandoned the idea of an app altogether, and pivoted to websites. Not because the task was too challenging (okay, maybe a little) but because websites can be accessed without an App Store, on any device.

Why Freelance?

Tell people that you're a freelancer and they think you're just being clever about being unemployed. The truth is that for organizations of a certain size, freelancers offer the best value and balance bertween an agency that might see the job as too small to be worth it, and someone who may have made a webpage or two in the past by may lack knowledge of modern standards or professionalism.

I work to keep informed of the latest techniques and bring that knowledge to each and every project I take on, giving you piece of mind that when you hire me, you're hiring someone who has your back when it comes to your website. Let's work together.
