Everyone has an origin story. Here's mine...

I started in web development after I realized marketing was considered a commodity discipline, and decided I wanted to make things. So, I decided to start by building a weather app for my Android phone based on the DarkSky API. I estimated it would take me a weekto get something that worked, and another week to polish it to my liking. This would prove to be a naive estimate.

After taking a week to install the Eclipse environment and the Android SDK, and inventing a few new profanities along the way, I abandoned the idea of an app altogether, and pivoted to websites. Not because the task was too challenging (okay, maybe a little) but because websites can be accessed without an App Store, on any device. I also wanted to take advantage of not having to start completely from scratch with web development, having some experience making edits to a MySpace page back in the days when they were a requirement for teens the way smartphones are today. After spending the next year learning about responsive web development, I decided I wanted to make it my career.

I work hard to keep informed of the latest techniques and bring that knowledge to each and every project I take on, giving you piece of mind that when you hire me, you're hiring someone who is up to date on the latest in modern web development.

Why Freelance?

Tell people that you're a freelancer and they think you're just clever about being unemployed. The truth is that for organizations of a certain size, freelancers offer the best value and balance between an agency that might see the job as too small to be worth it, and someone who may have made a webpage or two in the past by may lack knowledge of modern standards or professionalism.
